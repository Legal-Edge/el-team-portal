## FEDERAL — Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act (15 U.S.C. §2301–2312)

- **Statute**: 15 U.S.C. §2301 et seq. (enacted 1975); FTC implementing regs 16 C.F.R. Part 700–703
- **Coverage**: Any consumer product (including vehicles) sold with a written warranty
- **Standard**: "Reasonable number of attempts" — no specific number defined in statute; FTC guidance suggests 3–4 attempts or 30+ days as reasonable baseline
- **Defect Standard**: Must substantially impair use, value, or safety
- **Interaction with State Law**: Consumer may pursue BOTH federal and state claims simultaneously; state law prevails if more favorable to consumer
- **Attorney Fees**: Prevailing consumer entitled to costs + attorney fees (15 U.S.C. §2310(d)(2))
- **Arbitration**: Manufacturers may require informal dispute resolution first (16 C.F.R. Part 703) but cannot compel binding arbitration
- **Remedies**: Repair, replacement, or refund; damages for breach of warranty
- **Key Nuance**: Mag-Moss does NOT set specific attempt counts — courts look at totality of circumstances. A single catastrophic defect can qualify. More consumer-friendly than most state laws on safety defects.
- **Implied Warranty**: Also covers implied warranty of merchantability — vehicle must be fit for ordinary purpose of transportation

## ALABAMA
- **Statute**: Ala. Code §8-20A-1 et seq.
- **Vehicles Covered**: New motor vehicles (passenger cars, trucks, vans, motorcycles) — NOT used
- **Repair Attempts (same defect)**: 3 attempts
- **Safety Defect Threshold**: 1 attempt if likely to cause death or serious bodily injury
- **Days Out of Service**: 30 cumulative days
- **Warranty Window**: 24 months or 24,000 miles from delivery, whichever first
- **Notice Required**: Written notice to manufacturer required before filing
- **Arbitration Required**: Must use state-certified arbitration if available
- **Remedies**: Repurchase or replacement; attorney fees to prevailing consumer
- **Key Nuances**: Alabama requires direct manufacturer notice. Mileage deduction on repurchase for use.

## CALIFORNIA
- **Statute**: Cal. Civ. Code §1793.22 (Tanner Consumer Protection Act); Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act §1790 et seq.
- **Vehicles Covered**: New AND used vehicles (if still under original manufacturer warranty); leased vehicles; purchased for personal, family, or household use
- **Repair Attempts (same defect)**: 4 attempts (creates rebuttable presumption)
- **Safety Defect Threshold**: 2 attempts if nonconformity likely to cause death or serious bodily injury
- **Days Out of Service**: 30 cumulative calendar days (can be extended only if repairs impossible due to conditions beyond manufacturer control)
- **Warranty Window**: 18 months or 18,000 miles from delivery, whichever first (for presumption); underlying warranty period for Song-Beverly claims
- **Notice Required**: Direct manufacturer notice required ONLY if manufacturer conspicuously disclosed this requirement in warranty/owner's manual. If not disclosed, notice not required.
- **Arbitration Required**: Must exhaust manufacturer's qualified third-party dispute resolution process if timely notice was provided; CANNOT be required if notice was untimely or consumer would be prejudiced
- **Remedies**: Replacement or full restitution (purchase price + collateral charges + finance charges + incidental damages - reasonable use offset); attorney fees mandatory if consumer prevails
- **Key Nuances**: 
  - Strongest consumer lemon law in the nation
  - "Reasonable use" deduction = (miles at first repair attempt / 120,000) × purchase price
  - Covers boats, electronics, appliances in addition to vehicles
  - Active duty military transferred to CA can use CA law even if purchased out of state
  - Civil penalty up to 2× actual damages if manufacturer willfully violated
  - Presumption is rebuttable — consumer can still win even without meeting thresholds
  - Used vehicles covered if still under original manufacturer warranty

## FLORIDA
- **Statute**: Fla. Stat. §681.10 et seq. (Florida Motor Vehicle Warranty Enforcement Act)

  - 15-day OOS threshold is lowest of any state — very favorable to consumers
  - State arbitration board is free and binding on manufacturer
  - 10-day cure period after notice
  - Manufacturer must pay attorney fees if consumer prevails

## ANALYSIS ENGINE DESIGN RECOMMENDATIONS

### Tier 1 — Must Have
1. **Per-state threshold lookup** (all 50 states + DC + Federal)
2. **Defect grouping** — cluster RO complaints by defect category (semantic similarity)
3. **Attempt counter per defect group** — count ROs where same defect appears
4. **Days OOS calculation** — sum of (repair_date_out - repair_date_in) across all ROs
5. **Window verification** — check if repairs fall within (purchase_date + time_limit) AND (purchase_mileage + mileage_limit)
6. **Safety defect detection** — keyword list: brakes, steering, stall, fire, airbag, seatbelt, acceleration
7. **Dual-track**: run state law AND federal Mag-Moss simultaneously; report stronger track
8. **Notice status flag** — has manufacturer been formally notified?

### Tier 2 — Should Have
9. **Reasonable use deduction calculator** — for repurchase value estimates
10. **Arbitration requirement flag** — states requiring it before suit (TX, IL, CA conditionally)
11. **Missing data flags** — mileage gaps, missing purchase date, missing dates
12. **Confidence score** — how complete is the data vs. what's needed for analysis

### Tier 3 — Nice to Have
13. **Multi-defect recurrence analysis** — same vehicle, different defects, still qualify
14. **Time-gap analysis** — defects reappearing after "repair" (same defect, different visit)
15. **Manufacturer notification tracker** — draft notice if not yet sent
